Computer history

A computer is a digital electronic machine that can be programmed to carry out sequences of arithmetic or logical operations (computation) automatically. Modern computers can perform generic sets of operations known as programs.
The abacus was initially used for arithmetic tasks. The Roman abacus was developed from devices used in Babylonia as early as 2400 BC.
Charles Babbage, an English mechanical engineer and polymath, originated the concept of a computer. Considered the "father of the computer",[17] he conceptualized and invented the first mechanical computer in the early 19th century.
